To invest on Republic, you must understand the basics of crowdinvesting.
Please read and answer the questions below.

1. Risk

Investing in startups is very risky. You should only invest an amount you can afford to lose completely without changing your lifestyle. Learn more

2. Limited transfer

It may be difficult to transfer the securities acquired on Republic (whether Crowd SAFE, Token DPA, or other), In addition, there are federal restrictions on selling, trading, or transferring the securities for the first 12 months and potentially additional state and foreign restrictions. Some securities may not allow transfers altogether Learn more

I understand that it may be difficult to transfer my investments.

3. Diversification

Crowdfunding investing is highly speculative and every investment may result in a loss. By investing small amounts across multiple deals, you can reduce your risk compared to a large investment in a single company. Learn more

I understand that it's safer to split money across many investments across asset classes.

4. Cancellation

You can cancel your investment up to 48 hours before the deal deadline. After that, your investment will be final, and you will not be able to get your money back. Learn more

I understand that I can't cancel after the 48-hour cancellation deadline.

5. Limits

Generally investors are limited to $2,200 per year - depending on their net-worth and income, this limit may go up to $107,000 - across all Title lll offerings. Learn more

I understand that I can't invest more than my limits allow.

6. Research

Do your own research. Read the documents provided by each company. Get independent legar, accounting, financial advice. If you have any questions or need more information, ask the company in the discussion section. Learn more
